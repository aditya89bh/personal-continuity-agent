# Market Research Plan

## Research Goal

Identify where continuity failure creates enough pain that users, developers, or organizations would care about a Personal Continuity Agent.

The goal is not to study generic AI memory. The goal is to understand where long-term coherence, identity persistence, and unresolved context matter.

## Core Question

Where does loss of continuity create meaningful personal, professional, or operational cost?

## Research Tracks

### 1. Existing AI Memory Products

Study products and frameworks that claim to provide memory, personalization, or persistent context.

Categories:

- AI companions
- personal knowledge systems
- AI workspaces
- agent memory infrastructure
- wearable memory systems
- note-taking and second-brain tools

Evaluation dimensions:

- what they remember
- what they forget
- how memory is retrieved
- whether identity is modeled
- whether reflection exists
- whether temporal reasoning exists
- user trust and privacy model
- onboarding friction

### 2. User Pain Research

Investigate segments where continuity matters:

- founders and executives
- coaches and therapists
- healthcare professionals
- industrial operations teams
- knowledge workers
- students and researchers
- creators and solopreneurs

For each segment, identify:

- continuity failures
- current workaround
- frequency of pain
- severity of pain
- willingness to pay
- privacy sensitivity
- product wedge

### 3. Technical Landscape

Study technical approaches:

- vector memory
- graph memory
- event sourcing
- episodic memory
- semantic memory
- reflection loops
- salience scoring
- memory compression
- temporal knowledge graphs

### 4. Strategic Positioning

Compare possible repo directions:

- personal AI continuity engine
- developer framework for agent memory
- cognitive architecture research repo
- enterprise continuity infrastructure
- embodied AI continuity layer

## Research Output

The research phase should produce:

- competitor matrix
- use-case matrix
- technical architecture map
- positioning thesis
- first demo selection

## Research Timebox

Recommended timebox: 7 to 10 days.

The project should move quickly from research into prototype implementation.
