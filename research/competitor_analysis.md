# Competitor Analysis

## Purpose

This document analyzes existing products and frameworks related to AI memory, personalization, agent memory, and continuity.

The goal is to identify what they solve, what they miss, and where a continuity-first architecture can be differentiated.

## Evaluation Matrix

| Product / Framework | Category | What It Remembers | Continuity Depth | Identity Modeling | Reflection Loops | Temporal Reasoning | Key Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mem0 | Agent memory infrastructure | User facts and preferences | Medium | Limited | Limited | Limited | More memory layer than continuity engine |
| Zep | Agent memory infrastructure | Chat history and summaries | Medium | Limited | Some summarization | Limited | Strong infra, weaker human continuity thesis |
| LangChain Memory | Developer framework | Conversation context | Low | No | No | No | Mostly implementation utility |
| Replika | AI companion | Personal interactions | Medium | Some persona modeling | Unclear | Limited | Companion-first, not architecture-first |
| Character.AI | AI companion | Character/user interactions | Low to medium | Persona-focused | Limited | Limited | Entertainment orientation |
| Notion AI | Workspace AI | Documents and workspace context | Low | No | Limited | No | Workspace retrieval, not personal continuity |
| Reflect / Obsidian / Capacities | PKM tools | Notes and linked knowledge | Medium | User-driven | User-driven | Limited | Human-maintained, not agentic continuity |
| Limitless AI | Wearable memory | Meetings and conversations | Medium | Limited | Summaries | Limited | Capture-heavy, continuity-light |

## Differentiation Hypothesis

Most existing systems focus on one of three things:

1. storing memories
2. retrieving context
3. summarizing past interactions

A Personal Continuity Agent should focus on:

- long-term identity evolution
- unresolved loops
- goal drift
- salience and forgetting
- temporal behavior patterns
- reflection-driven self-model updates
