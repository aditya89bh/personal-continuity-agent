# Use Cases

## Purpose

This document maps where continuity failure creates real user or organizational pain.

The goal is to identify strong demo scenarios and possible future product wedges.

## Use Case Matrix

| Segment | Continuity Failure | Current Workaround | Pain Severity | Product Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founders / Executives | Strategy, decisions, and priorities get fragmented across weeks | Notes, docs, calendar, memory | High | Strategic continuity agent |
| Coaches / Therapists | Longitudinal emotional patterns are hard to track between sessions | Session notes | High | Reflective continuity assistant |
| Healthcare | Patient history becomes fragmented across providers and visits | EHR notes | Very High | Patient narrative continuity, regulated |
| Industrial Operations | Shift knowledge, exceptions, and machine context get lost | Logs, verbal handoffs, spreadsheets | High | Operational continuity layer |
| Knowledge Workers | Project context disappears across meetings and tools | Docs, Slack, Notion | Medium | Project continuity assistant |
| Researchers | Research direction and unresolved questions drift over time | Zotero, notes, docs | Medium | Research continuity agent |
| Creators / Solopreneurs | Ideas, drafts, audience signals, and creative identity fragment | Notes, content calendars | Medium | Creative continuity agent |

## Strongest Initial Wedges

### 1. Founder Continuity

A founder-facing demo can show how the agent tracks decisions, priorities, contradictions, and strategic drift.

### 2. Research Continuity

A research demo can track learning goals, open questions, paper summaries, and evolving hypotheses.

### 3. Industrial Continuity

An operations demo can connect the project to robotics, shift memory, machine exceptions, and embodied AI.

## Recommended First Demo

Start with a synthetic founder/researcher timeline.

Why:

- easy to simulate
- emotionally intuitive
- strong portfolio story
- connects to memory agents and AGI research
- avoids regulated domains early

## Demo Candidate

A user starts with three goals:

1. build an AGI research practice
2. publish weekly essays
3. finish a prototype repo

Across simulated events, the system detects:

- one goal is progressing
- one goal is repeatedly abandoned
- one identity theme is strengthening
- one unresolved loop keeps returning

The continuity agent then generates a recovery brief.
